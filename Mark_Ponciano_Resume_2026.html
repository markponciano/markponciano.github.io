Finance operations for a multi-venue events caterer, and the systems that run them

  • Built the operating platform: designed a MySQL schema I own as the single source of truth, with GoHighLevel, WordPress, Brevo, CloudConvert, Google Calendar and Xero all mirroring it, so a booking flows from enquiry to quote to invoice to payment with no re-keying. PHP and JavaScript, built with AI coding assistants.
  • Scheduled reconciliation pipeline: built an Apps Script pipeline for matchday payroll and sales that ingests form submissions on a timer, resolves inconsistent names to a canonical roster, de-duplicates with audit logging, rate-calculates pay, and reconciles hours against the posted schedule with variance and duplicate-payment flags. Cut about half a day of manual work per event.
  • Reconciliation and controls: reconciled the weekly P&L's cost configuration against Xero and surfaced about £80k a year in unaccounted costs, so profit had been overstated by the same. Added POS analytics and a keep-or-switch supplier cost analysis.

Bookkeeper, Confidential Multi-Practitioner Medical Clinic (Remote, AU)Jan 2025 – Dec 2025

40-practitioner superclinic: reconciliation and billing

  • Reconciliation pipeline: built an Apps Script pipeline reconciling ~15,000 transaction lines a month from Best Practice against the GPs' bank statements. Normalized the export first, then matched across a dozen payment rails (EFT, Medicare, DVA, WorkCover, HICAPS, credit card, deposits, cash): ~80% matched outright, ~15% pre-matched, and the hard ~5% flagged for review, including WorkCover records with no direct reference to match on. Ran monthly for the year, then handed it over with a walkthrough.
  • Revenue assurance: during a billing-model change that occasionally double-paid providers, cross-referenced the payments export against the service export to pin each provider's true overpayment, about $8K across 40 accounts, and recovered it in staged installments rather than clawing it back in one hit.
