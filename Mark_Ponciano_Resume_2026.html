Finance operations for a multi-venue events caterer, and the systems that run them

  • Built the operating system: designed and built a live, database-backed system that runs the company's bookings, quoting, invoicing and payments from end to end, wired to Xero, email and calendar.
  • Quote-to-invoice automation: before the company even had a CRM, built its first automated quote-to-invoice pipeline in Make.com, connecting Monday.com, Xero and CloudConvert so enquiries became invoices on their own.
  • Matchday finance automation (fast win): within three weeks of joining, built a multi-page living Google Sheet that handled stadium food-sales intake and subcontractor matchday payroll automatically, changing how the team ran matchday finance.
  • Reconciliation and controls: reconciled the weekly P&L's cost configuration against Xero and found about £80k a year (annualised) in costs the model was missing, so profit had been overstated by the same. Also ran POS analytics on discounts and voids, and delivered a keep-or-switch supplier cost analysis.
Bookkeeper, Confidential Multi-Practitioner Medical Clinic (Remote, AU)Jan 2025 – Dec 2025

Multi-provider ledger management

  • Revenue assurance: during the clinic's billing-model transition, the overlap of old and new methods occasionally double-paid providers. I cross-referenced the payments export against the service export to pin each provider's true overpayment, about $8K across 40 accounts, then set up a painless installment recoupment rather than a lump-sum clawback.
  • Reconciliation and controls: built reconciliation scripts that matched or pre-matched about 95% of transactions, so manual review could focus on the ~5% high-risk items (WorkCover QLD, icare NSW, patient refunds, HICAPS variances). Kept a daily rhythm, with weekly variance reporting and monthly ledger sign-off.
