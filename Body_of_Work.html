Capability 1 — Systems architecture & production engineering

I designed and shipped a live production platform, solo.

Not a prototype. Real users, real bookings, real money, tested and gated.

The "spine" — DB-backed operational engineJun 19 – Jul 8, 2026Live

A PHP 8.2 domain system (~50 classes) that replaced a SaaS CRM's automation layer. Bookings are records; state is derived from facts (quote accepted → op-form in → invoiced → paid) rather than hand-set. Deployed as WordPress mu-plugins. This is the company's live nervous system.

Big-bang production flipExecuted Jul 4, 2026Live

Migrated a live revenue path off its existing platform with an ordered, per-step-reversible runbook and a ~130-check pre-flight gate battery. Zero downtime. Senior-grade release engineering.

Test suite + 5-stage CI pipelineLive

358 PHPUnit tests including an end-to-end lifecycle regression (capture → quote → accept → op-form → invoice → pay → confirmed, asserting the money at each step). Every PR gated on lint, a custom collision check, PHPCS (PSR-12), PHPStan, and the full test run.

Migration-based schema (26 migrations)Live

26 numbered, idempotent, additive SQL migrations with a custom runner and a version ledger that caught real production drift the day it was introduced.

Production-incident hardening — double-invoice race fixPRs #153/#154 · Jul 7Live

Found and root-caused a real double-invoicing race (two Approve clicks → two Xero invoices), fixed it with an atomic DB claim, then audited and hardened every "once-only fact gates a side-effect" path. Concrete debugging depth and production ownership.

Observability — Cockpit & ObservatoryPRs #140–152 · Jul 6–7Live

An alert recorder with dedup + P1/P2 escalation wired into all seven cron sweeps, P1 email plus an external dead-man's-switch, and a live operator page with a heartbeat pill and plain-English activity feed.

Demonstrates: systems architecture · domain modelling · PHP OOP · database design · CI/PR discipline · automated testing · concurrency debugging · release engineering · operational resilience (SRE).

Capability 2 — Finance, data & reconciliation (the operating engine)

I run the weekly numbers a business depends on — and I automate them.

The recurring, reliable work owners never see and always rely on. ~43 deliverables, 10 types, an unbroken weekly rhythm since early 2026.

Weekly P&L validation6-week continuous backfill + weeklyRecurring

Rebuilds and validates the weekly profit-and-loss from source data (sales, COGS, waste, crew food, wages) against a fixed template, catching gaps and applying config-driven overrides.

VA payroll preparationWeekly since February 2026Recurring

Consolidates virtual-assistant hours per person, applies rates, converts USD→GBP at a captured exchange rate, formatted as a payroll-ready board import. The single most consistent stream in the record.

Matchday sales → Xero invoicing pipeline5+ fixtures, Apr–MayRecurring

Per football matchday: takes per-unit till sales, applies rent (25%) and admin charges, produces a board import, and generates a Xero-ready sales invoice with account codes and VAT. A complete source-to-invoice pipeline.

POS analytics — discounts, voids, price/COGS integrityWeekly, AprilRecurring

Breaks down Square discounts and voids by category against industry benchmarks, flags control gaps (e.g. anonymous discount codes at 30% of spend), and reviews supplier price changes for COGS-distorting unit-of-measure errors.

Break-even feasibility study (with statistics)Apr 2026Shipped

Cleaned 30 months / 173 Xero invoices to a defensible 9-invoice sample, derived average booking value with a Student's-t 95% confidence interval, modelled break-even, and honestly separated "self-funds easily" from "won't close the £500k gap." A near-academic evidence framework followed in Phase 2.

Demonstrates: financial modelling · P&L construction · reconciliation & controls · payroll processing · statistical analysis (confidence intervals, sensitivity) · data cleaning · COGS integrity · benchmarking — a bookkeeper's data-integrity discipline applied at scale.
