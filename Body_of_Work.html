How I work

I don't walk in and start building. I do the books and the day-to-day first, because that's where the real problems show themselves, in the reconciliations that never tie out and the jobs everyone dreads. Then I fix the most painful thing first, prove it works, and move on. Nothing fancier than it needs to be. And it's not only the books: any admin or backend job that runs on messy data or manual handoffs is fair game, financial or not.

This is for growing small and medium businesses whose back-office has fallen behind the rest of the business. It's not for enterprise, and it's not for anyone who needs a full-time software engineer. Sectors I know well: hospitality, healthcare, and events.

Money I've found in the books

Clawed back $8,000 from a billing switch that was double-paying providers2025, AU medical clinicShipped

A medical clinic of about 40 providers changed how it paid them partway through the year. For a few weeks the old and new methods overlapped, so some providers were paid twice. I cross-checked the payments export against the service log to pin down exactly who had been overpaid and by how much, totalling $8,000 AUD, then set it up as small installments through the weekly clinic-fee invoice so nobody took a sudden hit getting it back.

Found £80,000 a year that the weekly profit report was missingApr 2026Shipped

The weekly profit report was built on cost configurations the business had never fully accounted for, so it read higher than the real profit. I reconciled it against Xero side by side over four months and found about £1,540 a week, roughly £80,000 a year in real costs the model had been missing. Now the weekly number tells the truth.

The system underneath

GoHighLevel stayed the face, but the real work moved underneath2026Live

Everyone still clicks the same GoHighLevel screens they always did. Underneath, I built the part that actually runs the business: bookings, quoting, invoicing, payments. A booking's status now sorts itself out from what actually happened: whether the quote was accepted, whether the payment cleared. No one has to remember to move a card and click Approve.

Moved the live money path across without a minute of downtimeJul 2026Live

I switched the business onto the new system step by step, with a way back at every stage and about 130 checks run before flipping the switch. Nothing broke and nothing paused for customers. This is the kind of changeover most small businesses avoid, because it's usually where things go wrong.

Caught a bug making duplicate invoices, and got alerted before the owner noticedJul 2026Live

Two quick clicks on Approve could quietly create two Xero invoices for the same booking. I found it, fixed it the same day, and checked every similar spot so it can't happen elsewhere. Every change gets tested end to end before it can touch the live business, and if something fails overnight I get an alert before the owner ever sees it.
